About the Solar System

The Solar System is a vast collection of celestial bodies that orbit the Sun. It consists of:

  • The Sun – A giant ball of hot gases that provides energy to the entire system.
  • 8 Planets – Mercury, Venus, Earth, Mars, Jupiter, Saturn, Uranus, and Neptune.
  • Moons – Natural satellites that orbit planets (e.g., Earth's Moon, Jupiter’s Ganymede).
  • Asteroids & Comets – Rocky and icy bodies that move through space.

The Solar System is about 4.6 billion years old and exists within the Milky Way Galaxy. The planets revolve around the Sun in elliptical orbits, governed by gravity.
